About Mrs Burgess

Professional Background

Mrs Andrea Burgess has 25 years of experience caring for patients of all ages. She holds dual NHS positions at Hampshire Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust and University Hospital Southampton NHS Foundation Trust.

Having trained at Nottingham University Medical School, Mrs Burgess completed her specialist ENT training in London and Oxford before undertaking prestigious international fellowships.

International Fellowship Training

  • Otology Fellowship - Kresge Hearing Research Institute, University of Michigan, USA (2001-2002)
  • Paediatric and Head & Neck Fellowship - Royal Brisbane and Women's Hospital & Royal Children's Hospital, Queensland, Australia (2004-2005)

Leadership Roles

  • Associate Medical Director of Clinical Strategy, Hampshire Hospitals NHS Trust
  • Clinical Director of Head and Neck Surgery, Hampshire Hospitals NHS Trust
  • Paediatric Airway Co-Lead, University Hospital Southampton NHS Trust
  • Council Member, British Association of Paediatric Otolaryngologists
  • Council Member, British Laryngology Association

Qualifications & Memberships

  • BMed Sci, BM BS, FRCS (ORL-HNS)
  • GMC Number: 4041924
  • Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons
  • Member of ENT UK
  • Member of the Royal Society of Medicine

Publications & Awards

  • Dr Munro Prize for Innovation 2018 (for establishing the paediatric airway service)

Specialities

Main Subspecialty Interests

Paediatric ENT

Specialist care for children's ear, nose and throat conditions. As a mother of three, Mrs Burgess understands parental concerns and provides reassuring, child-friendly care.

Otology

Expert diagnosis and treatment of ear conditions, with specialist fellowship training from the renowned Kresge Hearing Research Institute.

General Adult ENT

Comprehensive ear, nose and throat care for adults, covering the full spectrum of ENT conditions.

Key Achievement: Co-founder of the Wessex Children's Airway Service
